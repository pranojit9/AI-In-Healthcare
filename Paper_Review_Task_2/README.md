# Targeted Research Synthesis: Privacy-Preserving AI using Federated Learning in Healthcare

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents a targeted research synthesis on one of the most critical challenges in modern AI-enabled healthcare: **privacy-preserving Federated Learning (FL)**. The study investigates how Federated Learning enables collaborative model training across multiple healthcare institutions without sharing sensitive patient data while addressing the trade-off between **privacy, model accuracy, communication efficiency, and clinical reliability**.

The research reviews recent state-of-the-art publications (2023–2026), identifies existing technical limitations, and proposes a future research direction for secure, scalable, and trustworthy AI-driven healthcare systems.

---

## 🎯 Research Objective

The primary objective of this project is to:

- Identify a specific research challenge in AI-based healthcare.
- Analyze recent State-of-the-Art (SOTA) solutions.
- Investigate technical limitations of existing approaches.
- Propose a potential hybrid solution for future research.
- Build a strong literature foundation for further academic studies.

---

## 🔬 Research Problem

The selected research problem is:

> **Balancing Privacy Preservation and Model Performance in Federated Learning for Healthcare Applications**

Although Federated Learning eliminates the need to share raw patient data, several challenges remain:

- Privacy leakage through model updates
- Performance degradation caused by Differential Privacy
- High communication overhead
- Limited scalability across heterogeneous hospitals
- Real-world deployment challenges

---

## 🏥 Clinical Importance

Solving this problem can significantly improve healthcare systems by:

- Protecting sensitive patient information
- Enabling secure collaboration between hospitals
- Improving AI-assisted diagnosis accuracy
- Supporting trustworthy clinical decision-making
- Reducing legal and regulatory risks

## 🔍 Technical Gaps Identified

The literature reveals several unresolved research challenges:

- Existing methods prioritize either privacy or accuracy but rarely optimize both.
- Communication overhead remains high in large-scale healthcare networks.
- Most approaches assume stable network environments.
- Limited evaluation on heterogeneous healthcare datasets.
- Resource-constrained IoMT devices are often overlooked.
- Explainability is rarely integrated with privacy-preserving Federated Learning.
- Real-world deployment and regulatory compliance remain open challenges.

---

## 💡 Proposed Research Direction

Based on the literature review, this project proposes a hybrid framework integrating:

- Adaptive Differential Privacy
- Secure Aggregation
- Communication Compression
- Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI)
- Dynamic Client Selection
- Edge AI Optimization
- Regulatory-aware Privacy Management

This hybrid approach aims to improve both privacy preservation and clinical prediction performance while maintaining communication efficiency.
